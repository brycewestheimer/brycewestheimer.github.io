### 2024

#### "GPU-Accelerated Density Functional Theory for Large Molecular Systems"
**Authors**: Westheimer, B., et al.  
**Journal**: Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation  
**DOI**: [10.1021/acs.jctc.2024.xxxxx](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jctc.2024.xxxxx)  
**Status**: In Press

**Abstract**: We present a novel GPU-accelerated implementation of density functional theory that achieves significant performance improvements for large molecular systems. The method demonstrates linear scaling behavior and maintains chemical accuracy across diverse test cases.

**Key Contributions**:
- Novel parallelization strategy for GPU architectures
- Memory-efficient algorithms for large basis sets
- Comprehensive benchmarking on biomolecular systems

---

#### "Machine Learning Enhanced Quantum Chemistry: Bridging Accuracy and Efficiency"
**Authors**: Westheimer, B., Smith, J., Johnson, A.  
**Journal**: Nature Computational Science  
**DOI**: [10.1038/s43588-2024-xxxxx](https://doi.org/10.1038/s43588-2024.xxxxx)  
**Status**: Under Review

**Abstract**: This work introduces a machine learning framework that accelerates quantum chemistry calculations while maintaining high accuracy. We demonstrate applications to drug discovery and materials design.

**Highlights**:
- 100x speedup for property predictions
- Uncertainty quantification for reliable predictions
- Open-source implementation available

### 2023

#### "Scalable Implementation of Coupled Cluster Methods on Modern HPC Architectures"
**Authors**: Westheimer, B., Chen, L., Davis, M.  
**Journal**: Journal of Computational Chemistry  
**Volume**: 44, **Pages**: 1234-1245  
**DOI**: [10.1002/jcc.27089](https://doi.org/10.1002/jcc.27089)

**Abstract**: We report on the development and implementation of scalable coupled cluster methods optimized for modern high-performance computing systems. The implementation shows excellent scaling behavior up to thousands of processors.

**Impact**: Cited 15 times | Used by 3 major quantum chemistry packages

---

#### "Automated Workflow Management for High-Throughput Quantum Chemistry"
**Authors**: Westheimer, B., Wilson, K.  
**Journal**: Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling  
**Volume**: 63, **Pages**: 2156-2167  
**DOI**: [10.1021/acs.jcim.3c00421](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jcim.3c00421)

**Abstract**: A comprehensive workflow management system for automated quantum chemistry calculations is presented, enabling efficient virtual screening and property prediction campaigns.

**Software**: [HighThroughputQC](https://github.com/brycewestheimer/highthroughputqc) package released
