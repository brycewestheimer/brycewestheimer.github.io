# Computational Chemistry

My work in computational chemistry focuses on fragment-based methods, multi-layer adaptive partitioning (MAP), and the development of accelerator-ready electronic structure software.

## Research Focus

- **Fragment-Based Quantum Chemistry** — Developing rigorous treatments of interfragment covalent bonds and subsystem-local SAPT methods
- **Multi-layer Adaptive Partitioning (MAP)** — Hierarchical partitioning strategies that balance accuracy and computational cost
- **GPU-Accelerated Electronic Structure** — Optimizing quantum chemistry kernels for heterogeneous CPU/GPU architectures
- **Open Scientific Software** — Maintaining collaborative, open-source infrastructure for computational chemistry

## Core Contributions

My research has contributed to:
- Rigorous treatments of covalent bonds in fragment-based methods (JPCA 2022)
- Scalable non-orthogonal molecular orbital expansions (JCP 2021)
- GAMESS modernization for new architectures (JCTC 2023)
- Accelerated effective fragment potential kernels for GPU deployment

## Collaborative Impact

Through collaborations with the Guidez and Lin groups at CU Denver and Ames National Laboratory, my work enables:
- Chemically realistic simulations using fragment-based methods
- Deployment on DOE-class supercomputing systems
- Accessible, open-source tools for the research community
